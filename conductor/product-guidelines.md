# Product Guidelines: Splitz

## Prose Style

Communication within Splitz should be **Concise & Technical**. We value the user's time and expertise.

- Avoid conversational filler; provide the information required for decision-making.
- Use precise terminology (e.g., "Settlement," "Principal," "Split Logic") consistently.
- For tooltips and help text, focus on the 'how-to' without unnecessary elaboration.

## Branding & Tone

The Splitz brand is **Modern & Minimalist**. The interface and overall experience should feel clean, fast, and uncluttered.

- **Visuals**: Use plenty of white space to reduce cognitive load.
- **Typography**: Choose clean, sans-serif fonts that are highly legible.
- **Color Palette**: Use a limited, intentional color palette where color is used to denote meaning (e.g., debt vs. credit) rather than just decoration.

## UX Principles

Our user experience is built on the foundations of **Absolute Clarity** and **Rich Data Visualization**.

- **Absolute Clarity**: There must be zero ambiguity in balance calculations. The path from "who paid" to "who owes what" must be transparent and traceable.
- **Data Visualization**: Complex financial data should be transformed into intuitive charts and dashboards. Visuals are the primary way users should understand their spending habits and group dynamics at a glance.
- **Frictionless Navigation**: The core tasks—adding an expense or checking a balance—should be achievable with minimal taps or clicks.

## Error Handling

Errors should be handled in a **Graceful & Subtle** manner.

- Instead of jarring modal interruptions, use inline notifications or subtle status changes that don't disrupt the user's flow.
- When an error occurs, provide a clear but brief explanation of the issue and, if possible, suggest an alternative or a simple corrective action.
- Prioritize the integrity of the financial data; if a calculation cannot be performed safely, inform the user concisely without overwhelming them with technical details.
